In this video, we physically dissected an Instant Pot air fryer lid and analyzed how its vanes contributed to its functionality.
For this presentation, while some of my groupmates disassembled the main functional parts of the air dryer lid, namely the heating element and the main vane, I took apart the main housing of the air fryer the rest of the group, uncovering the secondary air intake vane, the electrical control panel, and the motor that actuated all the impellers in the system. I took the dimensions of the vanes and made a CAD model of them. For the analysis, we discussed the mechanism of the air fryer in terms of fluid flow together, and I contributed the assumptions that the fluid has to operate under, for example the turbulent flow yielding a high Reynold's number.
